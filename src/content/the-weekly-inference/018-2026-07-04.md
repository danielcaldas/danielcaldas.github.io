## This Week

The US government's informal veto over frontier AI deployment — first visible when Claude Fable and Mythos were pulled offline, now formalized into a de facto licensing regime with geography-based access controls as the price of reinstatement — didn't slow Anthropic down so much as redirect it: the same week Claude Sonnet 5 shipped and Claude Science launched a direct pharmaceutical development play, Anthropic is simultaneously negotiating with regulators over who can access its models and competing with the pharma companies it's selling to. What makes this week distinctive is that every major story — export controls, energy grid ultimatums, humanoid robots crossing into homes and classrooms, AI agents becoming industrial infrastructure — reflects the same underlying condition: the demonstration phase is over, and the systems are now load-bearing, which means every failure, every watt, and every geopolitical decision carries real-world consequences that can't be walked back with a patch.
